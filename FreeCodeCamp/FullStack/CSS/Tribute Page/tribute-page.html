Robert Bosch

Technology for Life

Here is a timeline of Robert Bosch' life:

  • 1861: Birth of Robert Bosch on September 23 in Albeck near Ulm, Germany.
  • 1876: Completion of secondary school in Ulm and the beginning of an apprenticeship as a mechanic at Strauss in Ulm.
  • 1884: Employment at Thomas Edison’s company in New York, where he gained important experience in electrical engineering.
  • 1886: Founding of the "Workshop for Precision Mechanics and Electrical Engineering" in Stuttgart, laying the foundation for the Bosch company.
  • 1897: Development of the first high-voltage magneto ignition system for vehicles, which became the cornerstone of Bosch's success.
  • 1906: Introduction of the eight-hour workday in his company, a progressive step for the time.
  • 1910: Opening of a large production plant in Feuerbach near Stuttgart, significantly increasing the company’s capacity.
  • 1913: Bosch becomes one of the largest automotive suppliers in the world, with branches in the USA, England, and France.
  • 1921: Introduction of the first electric car horn, which became another bestseller.
  • 1926: Launch of the fuel injection system for diesel engines, revolutionizing engine technology.
  • 1932: Acquisition of Junkers & Co. GmbH, allowing Bosch to enter the heating technology sector.
  • 1937: Transformation of the company into a private limited company (GmbH) to secure its succession.
  • 1940: Support for resistance against the Nazi regime through financial aid and protective measures for persecuted employees.
  • 1942: Death of Robert Bosch on March 12 in Stuttgart at the age of 80.
  • 1953: Establishment of the Robert Bosch Stiftung, dedicated to his social and philanthropic endeavors.
